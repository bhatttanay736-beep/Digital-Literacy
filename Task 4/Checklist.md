# Social Media Do's and Don'ts

This checklist outlines the best practices for maintaining a professional and responsible digital footprint.

| **Do's**                                                                                                                           | **Don'ts**                                                                                                                      |
| :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **1. Think Before You Post:** Always consider if you would be comfortable with a future employer or professor seeing your content. | **1. Post Illegal Content:** Avoid sharing photos or videos of illegal activities, substance abuse, or unprofessional behavior. |
| **2. Privacy Settings:** Regularly review and update your privacy settings to control who can see your personal information.       | **2. Share Sensitive Info:** Never post your home address, phone number, student ID, or passwords publicly.                     |
| **3. Professional Networking:** Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni, professionals, and peers in your field.        | **3. Engage in Cyberbullying:** Avoid online arguments, "flaming," or posting disrespectful comments toward others.             |
| **4. Verify Sources:** Check the facts before sharing news or articles to avoid spreading misinformation.                          | **4. Share Without Credit:** Don't post someone else's work, photos, or ideas without tagging or mentioning the creator.        |
| **5. Positive Branding:** Share your projects, certifications, and academic achievements to build a strong digital reputation.     | **5. Ignore University Policies:** Don't post confidential information about your school, internships, or workplace.            |
