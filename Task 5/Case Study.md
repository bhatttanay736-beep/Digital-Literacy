# Case Study: UPI "Collect Request" Fraud

### **Overview of the Crime**

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves tricking users into authorizing unauthorized debits from their bank accounts. The most common method is the **"Collect Request Scam,"** where a fraudster exploits the "Request Money" feature of UPI apps like GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm. Instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly sends money to the criminal.

### **The Modus Operandi (Step-by-Step)**

1. **Target Selection:** Scammers find targets on marketplaces like OLX or Facebook. They pose as buyers for an item the victim is selling.
2. **Initial Contact & Trust:** The scammer calls the victim, expressing urgent interest. To appear "safe," they may claim to be an army officer or a bank employee.
3. **The Trap:** The scammer tells the victim, "I am sending you an advance payment. Just click the notification and enter your PIN to claim it."
4. **The Action:** The scammer sends a **Collect Request** (a "Pay" request) for a specific amount (e.g., ₹20,000).
5. **Authorization:** The victim, believing they are "receiving" money, enters their UPI PIN. This action immediately authorizes a debit from their own account.
6. **Laundering:** The funds are instantly moved through multiple "mule accounts" to make them untraceable.

### **Target Audience**

While anyone can be targeted, this crime frequently affects:

- **Students and young adults** selling items on second-hand marketplaces.
- **Small vendors** or home-business owners unfamiliar with the "Pay vs. Receive" distinction.
- **Elderly citizens** who are new to digital banking.

### **Consequences**

- **Financial Loss:** Immediate loss of savings, often ranging from ₹5,000 to several lakhs.
- **Psychological Impact:** Victims often feel deep shame or anxiety, leading to a loss of trust in digital financial systems.
- **Legal Hassle:** Reporting and attempting to freeze the transaction is time-consuming and often unsuccessful if not done within the "Golden Hour" (first 2 hours).
