# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist

Maintaining digital hygiene is essential for college students to protect their academic and personal data. Follow these 8 actionable tips to stay secure.

### **General Digital Safety**

- **1. Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Secure your university email, social media, and Google accounts with MFA. Even if someone gets your password, they cannot log in without the second code.
- **2. Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Avoid using names or birthdays. Use a combination of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across different sites.
- **3. Be Wary of Public Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into bank accounts or student portals using free public Wi-Fi (like at cafes or railway stations). Use a VPN or your mobile hotspot instead.
- **4. Think Before Clicking:** Do not click on links in SMS or emails claiming you have won a "scholarship" or that your "SIM card is blocked." These are common phishing tactics.
- **5. Regular Software Updates:** Keep your Windows/macOS and mobile apps updated. Updates often contain "security patches" that fix vulnerabilities hackers use to enter your device.
- **6. Check App Permissions:** Regularly review which apps have access to your camera, microphone, and contacts. Delete apps that you no longer use.

### **Financial & UPI Safety (India Specific)**

- **7. PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving:** Remember that you **never** need to enter your UPI PIN to _receive_ money. if someone asks you to enter your PIN to "claim a prize" or "receive a refund," it is a scam.
- **8. Verify UPI IDs Before Paying:** Always double-check the recipient's name in the UPI app (GPay, PhonePe, etc.) before hitting 'Pay.' Scammers often use names that look like official entities but are slightly misspelled.

---

### **How to Report Cybercrime**

If you or someone you know falls victim to a digital or financial scam, report it immediately to the authorities:

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (Available 24/7)
